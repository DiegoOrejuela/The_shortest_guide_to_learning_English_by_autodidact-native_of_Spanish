Without further ado, let's start!

The language


Knowing the fundamental aspects of what, in general terms, I would study, allowed me to have a general vision for Spanish and English, and surely for more languages when I decide to learn another.

Definition

Language is defined as a system of signs used by a community for its communication. The sign is an element with unity and informative load, which replaces the reference of other object or event. Therefore, language is a system of substitution of objects: of objects to which we assign meaning by others that we do not want to point out or that are not around us.
The signs can be as many as we have senses to capture and emit them. A classification of the signs in this sense is the following:

  • Sound — Ear
  • Visual — Vista
  • Smell — Smell
  • Taste — Taste
  • Touch — Touch

In this way, each type of sign has its materialization in languages, sometimes ones based on others. For the topic we are dealing with, for example, oral language in humans was the basis mainly for written language, among others.

Components

Every language (as far as I know) has the following components as pillars of its structure:


Semiotics | Relative to signs

from the Greek semeion (sign) and -tico (relative to)

ST





It deals with the internal conformation of the signs and their composition.



Semantics | Meaning of the signsSemiotics

from the Greek semantikos (relevant meaning of the signs)

SM





Relationship between sign and thing designates (meaning).



Grammar | Relationship between signs

from the Greek gramma (letter, written) and -tico (relative to)

GR





Structure and rules of the relationship between signs.

It is divided into two large areas such as morphology and syntactic.




Pragmatic | Use of the sign system

from the Greek pragma (fact or action) and -tico (relative to)

PR





Use of language, taking into account the factors that are not referenced in a purely formal study.


What component to take to start the study?

Although it is true that in our childhood we took the semiotic-semantic-pragmatic-grammar path, mainly because of the power of our sensory capacity in that period of our life, in adulthood reasoning ability is the protagonist of our learning. Build logical statements, abstract ideas and formulate concepts to understand what we face, allows us to assimilate and appropriate knowledge in a faster, more accurate and efficient. Therefore, the path I advise is that of grammar-semantics-semiotics-pragmatics.
